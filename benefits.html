Minimal Risk of Nuclear Fusion

Minimal Risk

Compared to nuclear fission, which is used in nuclear power plants and atomic bombs, nuclear fusion has minimal risk. Here are some reasons why:

  • No long-lived radioactive waste: Fusion reactors do not produce long-lived radioactive waste, which can remain dangerous for thousands of years. The radioactive waste produced by fusion reactors is short-lived, typically remaining dangerous for only a few decades.
  • No risk of meltdown: Unlike nuclear fission reactors, which rely on a chain reaction to produce energy and can potentially meltdown if not properly controlled, fusion reactors have minimal risk of meltdown. If the reactor loses power, the fusion reaction stops automatically.
