Clean Energy Benefits of Nuclear

Fighting Climate Change

Nuclear power is a low-carbon energy source that can help reduce greenhouse gas emissions and combat climate change. Unlike fossil fuels, nuclear power does not produce carbon dioxide or other greenhouse gases as part of the electricity generation process. According to the International Atomic Energy Agency, nuclear power plants can prevent the emission of about 2 billion tons of carbon dioxide per year if they replace fossil fuel plants.

Reliable Power Generation

Nuclear power plants can generate electricity around the clock, regardless of weather conditions or fuel availability. They are not affected by fluctuations in wind or sunlight, which makes them a reliable energy source for meeting base-load power demands. In addition, nuclear power plants have a long operating life, with many reactors operating for 40 years or more.

Cost-Effective Energy Source

Although nuclear power plants have high capital costs for construction, they have low operating costs and can generate electricity at a competitive price. According to the Nuclear Energy Institute, nuclear power plants in the United States generate electricity at an average cost of 2.14 cents per kilowatt-hour, which is lower than the cost of electricity from coal, natural gas, or renewable sources like wind or solar. In addition, nuclear power plants can operate for decades without needing to refuel, which reduces the overall cost of energy generation.
